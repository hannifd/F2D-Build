Tell the DVLA about your medical condition

Use this service to tell the DVLA about any medical conditions or disabilities that may effect your ability to drive.

Don’t worry

90% of drivers are allowed to retain their current licence or are issued a short period licence subject to medical review on renewal.

What you’ll need

  • your driving licence
  • your GP’s contact details
Start now
  • Surrendering your licence
  • Other ways to apply

There’s no legal age at which you must stop driving. You can decide when to stop, but medical conditions can affect your driving and might mean you have to give up your driving licence until you can meet the medical standards of fitness to drive again.

When you decide to stop driving or are advised by your doctor to stop you’ll need to tell DVLA and send them your licence.

Learn more about surrendering your licence.

By post

Please provide the following:

  • your full name and address
  • your driving licence number (or your date of birth if you do not know your driver number)
  • details about your medical condition

Send to
